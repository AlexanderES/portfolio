Bloom Operating System

A mobile operating system focusing on mindfulness

This project involved the creation of a prototype for a mobile operating system with its own version of common apps like clock, task, and mail applications. Our team decided to create an operating system to help users become more mindful of how they use their phones. Our main design principals were Intentional, Purposefully Simple, Interactive, and Informative. We Used Figma to create the wireframes/prototype. The project was broken up into six stages.

It’s elegant and clean. Personal and uplifting. And like a bouquet of flowers, it inspires growth.





Stage One

  • Coming up with a design language
  • Creating design principles
  • Creating mood boards
  • Conducting research on mindful applications
  • Choosing a device for the operating system

    • Our team started off thinking of apps we would like to use. We misunderstood our goal with the project at first. We thought about different apps like a parking helper, a cooking guide, and an app to help users discover food. After thinking about the common themes of the apps we thought about, we realized they all had something to do with exploration. Our original design language was Apollo, made to help encourage users to explore and discover their surroundings. During our next meeting when deciding on a device for our design language/operating system however, our design language changed. We decided to help users be more organized and mindful. This made our design language change to Bloom. Some of the main goals of the design language were to promote responsibility and help users be well informed. The devices we considered at the time were interactive panels, voice assistants, and even smart glasses. We still were unsure of which device to go with though.



    To help give us a better idea of which device would be the most helpful for promoting mindfulness, we researched different apps that had to do with mindfulness. By doing this, we also hoped to get a better idea to establish a layout for our lock screen. Below are two of the apps we looked at.

    Calm

  • 2017 App of the year by Apple
  • Category: Mindfulness
  • Offers features like “Sleep Stories” to help users fall asleep as well as ambient sounds like lake and rain noises
  • Take away: A well-designed app that encourages usability and relaxation. Paid subscription can be a problem though

    • Hi Locker

  • Rating: 4.1 stars (45K reviews)
  • Category: Lock Screen App
  • Offers three different layouts, classic, lollipop and ios. Has customizable fonts. Dedicates a screen to your calendar too

    • From here, we went on to make mood boards for the overall look and feel of our design language. We looked through websites like Dribbble for inspiration.


    With our mood boards made, our design language became Bloom, using the tagline "It's elegant and clean, personal and uplifting, and like a bouquet of flowers, it inspires growth."

    The goal of the design language was to promote healthy and informed living, using an interactive system that helps users live well.

    We targeted young adults, individuals wanting to improve their lifestyle, and smart-home device users.
